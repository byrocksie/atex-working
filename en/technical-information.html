Sole Construction

Principles that influence safety, durability, and stability.

Sole construction affects traction, wear, and energy absorption. In industrial settings, the outsole compound, midsole material, and bonding method determine how the shoe performs on contaminated or uneven surfaces.

Common construction approaches include direct injection and cemented bonding. Each method balances flexibility, repairability, and long-term resistance to chemicals and temperature variation.

Protective Toe Concepts

Impact protection defined by material and geometry.

Protective toe components are designed to withstand defined impact and compression tests. The choice of material affects weight, thermal behavior, and metal detection requirements.

Common materials include steel, aluminum, and composite options. Selection depends on site rules, temperature exposure, and comfort expectations during extended wear.

Conductivity Overview

Managing electrostatic behavior as part of risk control.

Footwear can be designed to control electrical resistance to reduce static build-up. Conductive, dissipative, and insulating categories describe broad ranges of resistance and are defined by testing standards and specific test conditions.

Performance depends on flooring, humidity, wear state, and overall system design. Selection should be aligned with site risk assessments and workplace procedures.

ATEX is a directive covering equipment and protective systems used in explosive atmospheres. Footwear suitability requires a site-level evaluation and does not imply product certification.

Applicable Standards

Clear context for procurement and compliance teams.

EN ISO 20345 specifies requirements for safety footwear with protective toe caps and includes baseline performance criteria along with optional properties. Compliance is demonstrated through testing to the standard.

Related standards, such as EN ISO 20346 and EN ISO 20347, define protective and occupational footwear categories. Always confirm the applicable standard for your site and use case.

Standards & Definitions

Clear terms for technical alignment.

Definitions are provided for general guidance and should be interpreted alongside your site procedures and the applicable standard version.

EN ISO 20345

Standard defining minimum requirements and test methods for safety footwear with protective toe caps. It outlines baseline performance and optional properties verified through testing.

Conductive Footwear (CD)

Footwear designed to provide low electrical resistance so electrostatic charge can dissipate under controlled conditions. Suitability depends on the workplace risk assessment.

Antistatic vs Conductive

Antistatic footwear provides a controlled, higher resistance path to reduce static build-up while limiting shock risk. Conductive footwear provides lower resistance for faster dissipation. Selection should match site requirements.

Protective Toe Concepts

Protective toe components are engineered to meet impact and compression tests defined by standards. Material and geometry influence weight, thermal behavior, and detection requirements.

Sole Performance Basics

Outsole compound and tread affect slip resistance, abrasion, and exposure to oils or heat. Midsole design influences cushioning and energy absorption, with performance affected by wear and surface conditions.
